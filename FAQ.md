# DropSnooze — FAQ

## What does DropSnooze do?

It gives "later" a button. Drag any file, link, note, or image onto the menu-bar icon, hold for a moment, and pick a time — Tonight, Tomorrow, 2 Hours, or a custom one. The item disappears, and at that time it comes back and opens the actual thing, not just a reminder about it.

## How is it different from shelf apps like Yoink or Dropover?

Those are great at holding things while you move them around. DropSnooze answers a different question: *when should this come back?* Shelves hold; DropSnooze brings things back at the time you picked, and opens them.

## Why not just use Reminders or a to-do app?

Turning "deal with this later" into a task is about six steps: open the app, new item, paste, pick a date, pick a time, save. DropSnooze is one gesture — and at the end you get the file or page itself reopened, not a line of text telling you it exists.

## Does it copy or move my files?

No. Files stay exactly where they are. DropSnooze remembers them with security-scoped bookmarks and reopens the original in place. Deleting an item in DropSnooze never deletes your file (stashed copies go to the Trash, recoverable).

## What about privacy? Does it connect to the internet?

The app makes zero network requests — there is no networking code in it. No account, no sync, no analytics. The App Store privacy label is "Data Not Collected", and the app runs in the App Sandbox. (Purchases go through Apple's StoreKit, which is system-mediated.)

## How much does it cost?

Free to download and try — you can stash 5 items before it asks you anything. Unlocking unlimited stashes is a one-time $7.99 purchase. No subscription, ever.

## Why isn't it available in the EU or mainland China?

As a solo developer I currently don't meet the EU Digital Services Act "trader" disclosure requirements (which would require publishing a personal address and phone number). Mainland China requires separate arrangements. I may revisit both later.

## What are the system requirements?

macOS 14 (Sonoma) or later. Universal binary — runs natively on Apple silicon and Intel Macs.

## How do refunds work?

Purchases are handled by Apple. You can request a refund at [reportaproblem.apple.com](https://reportaproblem.apple.com).
